\subsection{\color{red}The Universal Property}

Suppose a function, $g$, that satisfies the following two equations:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcl}
  $g\;\;\emptylist $ & $=$ & $\mathcal{Z}$\\
  $g\;\;(x:xs)     $ & $=$ & $f\;\;x\;\;(g\;\;xs)$
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{center}
  \begin{tabular}{lcl}
    $\begin{array}{lcl}
      g\;\;\emptylist  &= & v   \\
      g\;\;(x:xs)      &= & f\;\;x\;\;(g xs) 
    \end{array}$
    & $\Leftrightarrow$
    & $g = fold\;\;f\;\;v $
  \end{tabular}
\end{center}
